# Direct Reports and Time Card Issues

## Issue Pattern
Direct reports are not submitting their time cards on a regular basis, leading to approval delays and potential issues with leave tracking and reporting.

## Common Symptoms
- Direct reports not submitting time cards
- Approval delays for time cards
- Leave tracking and reporting issues
- Rejected or cancelled time cards due to incomplete information

## Possible Causes
- Incomplete or missing user input
- Technical issues with the system or time card submission process
- Lack of reminders or notifications for direct reports
- Insufficient training or guidance for direct reports on submitting time cards

## Troubleshooting Steps

1. Check if the direct report has submitted their time card. If not, remind them to do so.
2. Verify that the user input is complete and accurate. Ensure that all required fields are filled in correctly.
3. Investigate any technical issues with the system or time card submission process. Check for errors, warnings, or errors logs.
4. Consider sending a reminder or notification to direct reports about the importance of submitting their time cards on a regular basis (e.g., weekly).
5. Review and update established processes and procedures for time card submission and approval.

HR Support Team

## Priority Guidance
This issue is typically considered High priority, as it can cause delays in leave tracking and reporting, and may impact the accuracy of HR data.
